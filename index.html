Hennes & Mauritz (H&M)

Role: Sales Advisor (Part-Time)
Duration: 5 years from 2017 to 2024
(combined across multiple periods)

Main Responsibilities:

During my time at H&M, I gained extensive experience across multiple aspects of retail operations.
I provided great customer service, through assisting customers with inquiries, and creating a positive shopping experience.
I also handled transactions efficiently and accurately as a cashier, while maintaining a tidy and organized department
to create a pleasant shopping environment. In addition, I managed the fitting rooms, ensuring cleanliness and smooth customer flow.
I was actively involved in inventory management, including restocking and maintaining stock accuracy, as well as contributing to
visual merchandising by arranging displays to enhance store presentation. Beyond these operational tasks, I also
mentored new part-time staff, sharing knowledge on store processes and best practices in customer service.

I have served as a Sales Advisor at the then-Waterway Point Outlet for 2.5 years.
Throughout my time there, I have gained valuable experience in customer service and retail operations.
I was entrusted by my managers to train some of the new part-timers, which helped me develop my leadership skills.
Furthermore, I took the initiative to carry out Visual Merchandising and Inventory Management tasks.

I left the Waterway Point Outlet in September 2019 due to my National Service enlistment in October 2019.

In September 2017, my store manager in the then-Waterway Point outlet gave me the opportunity to support
the then-ION Orchard outlet with the launch of the H&M's cosmestic line, H&M Beauty Department, for 3 days.
ION Orchard outlet is one of the top outlets in Singapore, so it was a valuable experience to understand
how they operated their store, and the difference between the store located in the city
and the heartland store like Waterway Point.

In September 2018, I was given the opportunity to support the Tampines Mall Outlet,
the only outlet in the Eastern region of Singapore.
In my 3 days of support, I was mainly involved in assisting with the store operations
and customer service during the busy period.
This experience allowed me to adapt quickly to a new environment,
and understand the dynamics of a different, yet similar, store setting.

In October 2021, I decided to rejoin H&M during my first year of undergraduate studies.
Working in Orchard Building Flagship Outlet provided me with the opportunity to work in the biggest H&M store in Singapore.
It was a brand new experience for me as the country was in the midst of the covid pandemic.
On top of the daily store operation tasks such as customer service, cashiering, and inventory management,
I also carried out Safe Entry tasks such as checking customers' vaccination status.
Apart from that, I was working in the Ladies and H&M HOME departments, which are the front-facing departments of the store
as both departments are located at the first floor of the store.
Through the 6 months of working there, I have gained valuable experience in working in the flagship store.

In September 2022, I continued my stint with H&M in the NEX Outlet, where I continued to build on my retail experience.
I was involved in various store operations, including customer service, cashiering, and inventory management.
I was also given the opportunity to train new part-timers, which allowed me to further develop my leadership skills.
This role allowed me to further develop my skills in a fast-paced retail environment.
I left the NEX Outlet in April 2023 to embark on my Internship with Workforce Singapore (WSG).

In December 2023, I rejoined H&M in the NEX Outlet. As usual, I was involved in various store operations,
including customer service, cashiering, and inventory management.
As I hit my 5 years of service with H&M in May 2024, I was admitted into the H&M Incentive Program (HIP),
which recognizes and rewards long-serving employees for their commitment and contributions to the company.
This program made me feel valued and appreciated for my long service with the company.
I left the company in December 2024 due to my final semester of studies.
Overall, my time with H&M has been a rewarding experience, and I have learned a lot about retail operations and customer service.
I have also developed my leadership skills through training new part-timers and being entrusted with various responsibilities.
I am always open to new opportunities, and I look forward to rejoining H&M in the future if the opportunity arises.
